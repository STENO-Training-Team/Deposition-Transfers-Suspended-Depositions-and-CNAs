Deposition Transfers
Taking over a job from another videographer, either before it starts (Pre-Depo) or mid-deposition (Mid-Depo)
Most Common Mistake
Departing videographers forget to upload their OBS backup to the shared folder or label it incorrectly. The replacement vid can't complete the worksheet without these files!
Understanding Transfer Types
Pre-Depo Transfer: Taking over before deposition starts - handled like standard job with shorter notice (can clock in 15 min prior)

Mid-Depo Transfer: Taking over during in-progress deposition - Video Scheduling & Depo Support will call for availability confirmation first
Need to Transfer Out Mid-Deposition?
If you have a hard-stop or need someone to take your place during a deposition, contact #video-help at least one hour before you need to leave. In case of emergency, contact them as soon as possible.
Protocols for Transferring Out (Departing Vid)
  • Post all relevant info to the replacement in #depo-transfer Slack thread
  • Confirm replacement is in the room, assigned as host, and has started their OBS backup
  • If on record, announce the replacement during a break and introduce them to attorneys, CR, and witness
  • Save your work before pressing TRANSFER (you'll lose dashboard access immediately)
  • Confirm replacement can access the Worksheet before leaving
  • Continue running OBS backup until new vid fully takes over
  • For REA jobs: confirm all exhibits are uploaded prior to transfer
  • Label your OBS file: Job number - Witness Name - Part # - date - obs.raw
  • Upload backup to the shared folder: Backup Upload Folder
Protocols for Taking Over (Replacement Vid)
  • Monitor Slack in #depo-transfer for updates and instructions
  • Join depo room at communicated time and start OBS backup immediately
  • Coordinate with outgoing vid to confirm completed tasks (SC App walkthrough, CR preferences, etc.)
  • Clarify roles of all participants in the room
  • Wait for host transfer and verify worksheet access (In Progress tab → locate by job number)
  • Post your name, role, and phone number in room chat for participants
  • Update "Your Name" and "Your Email" fields in Job Information section
  • Attach outgoing vid's backup files from shared folder to your worksheet
  • Delete uploaded files from shared folder after attaching to worksheet
  • Click "Transfer Complete" button in Slack thread
Suspended Depositions & Protective Orders
Deposition temporarily halted so a party can seek a court order - often a protective order that blocks testimony to prevent harassment, embarrassment, or undue burden
When Counsel Disagrees About Suspending
If counsel disagrees about suspending, contact #video-help immediately to reach Carly Savar (General Counsel). Always follow attorneys' lead - continue on record if no agreement until protective order or agreement is reached.
What's the Difference?
Canceled/Rescheduled: Due to logistical issues (participant absence, missing documents)

Suspended: Specifically halted to seek court order, often after dispute between counsel regarding witness privacy or harassment
What Is a Protective Order?
  • A legal "stop button" that blocks testimony if an attorney believes answering would cause embarrassment, harassment, undue burden, or expense
  • Prevents the disclosure of certain information under certain circumstances
  • The magic words that halt proceedings: "I would like to go off the record so that we may seek a protective order"
  • Once these words are said, immediately stop taking testimony and go off the record
Venue-Specific Rules for Protective Orders
  • Texas: Both sides (Taking Attorney and Opposing Counsel) must agree to go off the record OR have a court order
  • California: If a lawyer suspends to seek a protective order (citing bad faith or harassment), you may go off the record once that lawyer exits and the CR goes off the record
  • Other States (General Rule): Either side's request to seek a protective order will stop the deposition immediately
When a Protective Order Is Requested
  • Immediately stop taking testimony from the witness
  • Do not ask any questions about case matters on the record after this statement
  • Go off the record immediately and suspend the deposition
  • Note: Counsel may still make statements about the Protective Order on the record afterward
  • Follow the CR's lead throughout the process
Videographer Actions for Protective Orders
  • Immediately announce: "WE ARE NOW OFF THE RECORD. THE TIME IS [TIME/TIMEZONE]"
  • Follow the CR's lead
  • If counsel requests to make statements on the record about the Protective Order, go back on the record to allow statements, then take the deposition back off the record
  • Notate on your worksheet in Review Job Information Additional Notes: "Counsel asked for a protective order at [TIME] and we immediately went off the record and ended the deposition, no read-off was said"
  • Facilitate everyone's departure from the meeting as usual
If a Protective Order Already Exists
  • If attorneys ask you not to take video orders because a protective order already exists, remain calm and wait for input from the CR
  • Advise counsel that Steno will comply with the protective order
  • Direct them to email the protective order to concierge@steno.com
  • Notate all relevant information regarding the protective order in your Video Worksheet for future reference
Handling Unclear Situations
  • If the situation is uncertain (e.g., one lawyer leaves and the other refuses to engage), immediately contact #video-help for guidance
  • If the CR has left and counsel acts unreasonably or in bad faith, it is acceptable to end the session
Post-Deposition Worksheet
  • Note "Deposition suspended to seek a protective order" on video worksheet
  • Add any relevant details in internal Videographer service notes box
  • Complete all other post-depo tasks as usual unless otherwise instructed
CNAs (Certificate of Non-Appearance)
Formal document certifying that a scheduled deponent failed to appear - used as evidence when filing motions
Critical Documentation Requirement
In the Additional Notes section of your worksheet, you MUST specify either "#CNA with video" or "#CNA no video". Just writing "#CNA" is not sufficient.
When Does a CNA Happen?
If opposing counsel or the deponent is absent past the deposition start time, it might be a CNA. Always confirm with the Taking Attorney on how to proceed.
CNA WITH Video Orders
  • Ask counsel if they want the full official read-on
  • If yes, read the script from your worksheet but OMIT the line about swearing in the witness (no witness present)
  • Instead close with: "Thank you, counsel, you may proceed."
  • If counsel doesn't want full read-on, adjust per their instructions
  • When counsel finishes, close with: "This concludes today's proceedings in the matter of [CASE NAME]. We are now off the record. The time is [HH:MM TIME ZONE]."
CNA WITHOUT Video Orders
  • Do NOT read the proceeding on the record (no official read-on)
  • Allow counsel to give their statement with the Court Reporter
  • Stay in remote meeting until all statements are complete and participants have left
Completing Your Worksheet
  • Select "Yes" for "Is this a CNA job?" (automatically updates service to CNA)
  • Minimum billable hours should be 1 hour
  • Add exhibits if presented (e.g., Notice of Deposition), otherwise check "No exhibits marked"
  • If counsel declined video, check "No orders requested"
  • In Additional Notes, specify "#CNA with video" or "#CNA no video"
  • If backup exists and no video was ordered, delete file from AWS
  • Complete remaining post-deposition tasks and submit worksheet
Important Reminders
• Do NOT read General Recommendations for CNAs
• Only post in #video-help if it was contentious or a rescheduled date was given
• Do NOT post directly in #scheduling for CNA results
